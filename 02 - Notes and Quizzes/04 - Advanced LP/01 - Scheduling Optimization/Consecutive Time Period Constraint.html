    I'd like to ask your opinion about how to model a constraint. The constraint can be either linear or non linear.

    CONTEXT: employee scheduling. NN employees can be assigned to MM shifts, each shift belongs to a day DD. I created a binary variable xn,m,dxn,m,d where xn,m,d=1xn,m,d=1 indicates that employee nn is assigned to shift mm at day dd (the dd index is useful for other constraints).

    CONSTRAINT TO MODEL: each employee prefers to work at least KK days in a row. (i.e. kk consecutive days with at least one shift per day)

    What I already did is to model a variable "working_day [n,d]" that tells if the employee n has at least a shift in the day dd.

    Any idea?

    According to your topic, you already have a binary variable xn,m,t∈{0,1}xn,m,t∈{0,1} that indicates whether employee nn is assigned to shift mm at day tt and a binary variable yn,t∈{0,1}yn,t∈{0,1} that indicates whether employee nn works at day tt or not. Let TT the total number of days.

    We can solve this problem with linear constraints as follows. Introduce an additional binary variable zn,t∈{0,1}zn,t∈{0,1} that indicates whether employee nn start a sequence of consecutive working days at day tt. A sequence of working days starts at day tt if the employee does work at day tt whilst not working at day t−1t−1, i.e., yn,t=1yn,t=1 and yn,t−1=0yn,t−1=0. Then we can introduce the constraint that a sequence of working days can only start if the following k−1k−1 days are also working days.

    zn,1=yn,1for zn,t≥yn,t−yn,t−1k⋅zn,t≤∑i=0k−1yn,t+ifor t=2,…,Tfor t=1,…,T−k+1First working dayFirst working dayAt least k consecutive working dayszn,1=yn,1for First working dayzn,t≥yn,t−yn,t−1for t=2,…,TFirst working dayk⋅zn,t≤∑i=0k−1yn,t+ifor t=1,…,T−k+1At least k consecutive working days
    The first constraint enforces zn,tzn,t to be 1 if employee nn starts working at day tt and the second constraint enforces zn,tzn,t to be zero if employee does not work at least the upcoming kk days (including the current day).
